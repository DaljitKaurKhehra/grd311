The Iconic Thanksgiving Appetizer: Green Bean Cassorole 

It just isn’t Thanksgiving without green bean casserole! This iconic dish is loved by famillies everywhere for its creaminess and savoriness, as well as the texture of the cooked veggie and crunchy onions. Don’t worry if you've never made this before—this dish from Delish is super simple. You only need 8 ingredients and about one hour to make it. I hope your is apron ready!   

  • 1 lb. green beans, trimmed
  • 6 tbsp. butter, divided
  • 1 onion, sliced into half moons
  • Kosher salt 
  • 8 oz. sliced mushrooms
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups of whole milk 
  • 1.5 cups of French's fried onions 
  1. Preheat oven to 350° degrees and prepare an ice bath
    1. In a large pot of boiling water, add green beans and cook until bright green, about 6 minutes.
    1. With a slotted spoon or tongs, quickly transfer green beans to ice bath to cool, then drain and transfer to a large bowl.
  2.  In a large ovenproof skillet over medium heat, do the following
    1. melt 2 tablespoons butter.
    2. Add onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender, about 5 minutes.
    3. Add mushrooms and season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring often, until mushrooms are golden, about 5 minutes more.
    4. Stir in garlic and cook until fragrant, 1 minute, then transfer mixture to green bean bowl.
  3. In the same skillet on medium heat, do the following:
    1. melt remaining 4 tablespoons butter
    2. Whisk in flour and cook until golden, about 2 minutes.
    3. Gradually whisk in milk and season with salt and pepper.
    4. Bring to a simmer and cook until thickened, about 4 minutes.
    5. Remove from heat, then add green bean mixture and toss until evenly combined.
  4.  Bake until warmed through and bubbling (takes about 30 minutes).
  5.  Top with fried onions and bake 5 minutes more.

Roasted Beet Hummus with Pita Chips

Here's a great idea for a delicious Thanksgiving appetizer: Beautifully purple-coloured beet hummus served with pita chips. This homemade hummus is so much better than any store bought one, and adds a nice splash of colour to the table! It’s really easy and quick to make (if you have a blender, it would take no more than 10 minutes). The beets give it a natural sweet note, while complimenting the tart tahini and pungent garlic added in. I highly recommend trying this hummus this year, as it has a ton of health benefits too, like Vitamin C!

  • 2 medium-sized beets
  • 400 g can chickpeas
  • 3 tablespoons tahini paste
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Black sesame to garnish
  • 1-2 Bags of Pita Chips 
  1. Preheat the oven to 400 F or 200 C
  2. Wrap each washed and dried beet in aluminum foil and roast for 1 hour. Pierce with a fork to check the readiness. Then let them cool and peel them.
  3. Combine roughly chopped beets with chickpeas, tahini paste, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, sea salt and black pepper in a food processor and blend until smooth, gradually adding the olive oil.
  4. Add extra salt and/or pepper for taste
  5. Transfer to a bowl and top with black sesame to garnish  
  6. Bake pita chips on 400 F or 200 C for 5 to 7 minutes or until golden and crispy. Serve with roasted beet hummus.

A Favourite Vegan Entrée: Roasted Butternut Squash Soup!

Cozy up with a bowl of warm, creamy butternut squash soup this Thanksgiving! This butternut squash soup is super creamy and full of rich roasted butternut squash flavor. It also makes for a great "make-a-day-ahead" dish for any time in the Fall or Winter season!  

  • 1 large butternut squash,  halved vertically and seeds removed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • ½ cup chopped shallot 
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, pressed or minced
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3 to 4 cups vegetable broth, as needed
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons butter, to taste
  • Handful of black beans, to top  
  1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit and line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the butternut squash on the pan and drizzle each half with just enough olive oil to lightly coat the squash on the inside (about ½ teaspoon each). Rub the oil over the inside of the squash and sprinkle it with salt and pepper.
  2. Turn the squash face down and roast until tender and completely cooked through, about 40 to 50 minutes (don’t worry if the skin or flesh browns—that’s good for flavor). Set the squash aside until it’s cool enough to handle, for about 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, in a large soup pot, warm 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat until shimmering (if your blender has a soup preset, use a medium skillet to minimize dishes.) Add the chopped shallot and 1 teaspoon salt. Cook, stirring often, until the shallot has softened and is starting to turn golden on the edges, about 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute, stirring frequently. Transfer the contents to your stand blender 
  4. Use a large spoon to scoop the butternut squash flesh into your blender. Discard the tough skin. Add the maple syrup, nutmeg and a few twists of freshly ground black pepper to the blender. Pour in 3 cups vegetable broth, being careful not to fill the container past the maximum fill line (you can work in batches if necessary, and stir in any remaining broth later).
  5. Make sure to securely fasten the lid. Blend on high (or select the soup preset, if available), being careful to avoid hot steam escaping from the lid. Stop once your soup is ultra creamy and warmed through. 
  6. If you would like to thin out your soup a bit more, stir in the remaining cup of broth.  
  7. Add 1 to 2 tablespoons butter or olive oil, to taste, and blend well. Taste and stir in more salt and pepper, if necessary.
  8. If your soup is piping hot from the blending process, you can pour it into serving bowls. If not, pour it back into your soup pot and warm the soup over medium heat, stirring often, until it’s nice and steamy. Top individual bowls with minimal black beans.

These are the Absolute BEST Thanksgiving Mashed Potatoes

You read that title right! The mashed potatoes in this recipe are the perfect combination of creamy and cheesy, and best of all, they're made with reduced fat cream cheese and light sour cream, meaning they're much healthier for your Thanksgiving guests! Readers have been consistently giving this recipe 5 star reviews, and I gurantee your famillies will love it too!     

  • 5 pounds of Yukon Gold Potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons Butter
  • 2 cups (15 oz) of Light Sour Cream
  • 8 ounces Reduced Fat Cream Cheese
  • 4 ounces Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 teaspoons Salt
  • ½ teaspoon Pepper
  1. Peel the potatoes. Chop into cubes and boil until tender.
  2. Drain potatoes and return to pot or place in the bowl of an electric mixer.
  3. Add butter, cheddar cheese, cream cheese, 1 cup sour cream, salt and pepper to the potatoes.
  4. Mix and mash either with a mixer or a hand masher.
  5. Taste test, and add more salt and pepper as necessary.
  6. Place in a buttered casserole dish and bake in the oven at 350-400 degrees. Bake until crispy on the bottom on edges - this takes about 30 minutes if you are putting the potatoes in the oven right away and about 1 hour if you are reheating the potatoes.

No Bake Apple Pie!

Short on time? Not too confident in the kitchen! This tasty, sweet no bake apple pies are here to save you this Thanksgiving! Their subtle sweet flavour and crunchy texture make this dessert an immediate hit - just read the multiple 5 star reviews!       

  • 1 package (0.3 ounce) sugar-free lemon gelatin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 
  • 1-3/4 cups water, divided 
  • 5 medium tart apples, peeled and sliced 
  • 1 package (0.8 ounce) sugar-free cook-and-serve vanilla pudding mix
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts 
  • 1 reduced-fat graham cracker crust (9 inches) 
  1. In a large saucepan:
    1. mix gelatin, cinnamon, nutmeg and 1-1/2 cups water.
    2. Add apples; bring to a boil. Reduce heat; simmer, covered, until apples are tender, about 5 minutes.
  2. In a seperate bowl:
    1. mix pudding mix and remaining 1/4 cup water; stir into apple mixture
    2. Cook until thickened, about 1 minute, stirring occasionally.
    3. Remove from heat; stir in nuts.
    4. Transfer to crust.
  3. Refrigerate at least 2 hours before serving.
